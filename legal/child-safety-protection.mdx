TIBS does not allow any content, behavior, or use of the platform that
exploits, harms, or endangers children. We maintain a strict **zero-tolerance
policy** toward any form of child exploitation or abuse.

Users of TIBS must not engage in activities including but not limited to:

- recording, submitting, sharing, or referencing any content that involves the
  exploitation or abuse of minors
- capturing or describing minors in a sexualized or inappropriate manner in
  any report, note, or submission
- using the platform to facilitate, promote, or coordinate any activity that
  may harm or endanger children
- engaging in or attempting any form of grooming, coercion, or inappropriate
  interaction involving minors
- using TIBS in any way that enables, supports, or conceals harmful behavior
  toward children

---

## Platform Use Expectations

TIBS is designed for responsible and lawful use.

All users must:

- use the platform only for its intended purpose
- avoid collecting, sharing, or describing personal information about
  individuals, especially minors
- refrain from any interaction or behavior that could put vulnerable
  individuals at risk
- disengage from any situation that may involve harm or unsafe conditions

---

## Monitoring and Enforcement

TIBS may use automated systems and manual review processes to detect and
prevent misuse of the platform.

Any violation of this policy may result in:

- immediate removal of content
- suspension or permanent termination of accounts
- restriction from future participation
- reporting to relevant authorities where required by law

---

## Reporting Concerns

If you encounter any content or behavior that may violate this policy, report
it immediately:

- Email: **[privacy@tisabs.com](mailto:privacy@tisabs.com)**
- Or through in-platform reporting tools (when available)

---

## Commitment

TIBS is committed to maintaining a safe environment for all users and the
public.

All users are expected to act responsibly, ethically, and within the scope of
lawful platform use.
