## Policy scope

The reporting policy governs how users may submit incident and service reports
through the TIBS platform.

The policy is designed to keep the reporting system useful for:

- commuter awareness
- transport safety transparency
- route and corridor visibility
- rider-facing service quality information

## Acceptable report categories

The source document allows reports tied to transport-related situations that may
affect:

- passenger safety
- ride quality
- service reliability
- transport awareness for other riders

Examples called out in the source include dangerous driving, unsafe vehicle
conditions, overcrowding, crew conduct affecting safety, incidents, service
disruptions, fare irregularities, unsafe stop conditions, and corridor safety
issues.

## Key operational rules

- reports should be made in good faith
- users should provide accurate context to the best of their knowledge
- reports should focus on observable transport events
- the reporting system must not be used for harassment, defamation, or false
  submissions
- personal information should be minimized

## Platform role

The source policy makes an important platform boundary explicit:

- TIBS is an information-sharing platform
- user reports are not automatically verified
- TIBS does not determine legal fault
- the platform is not a substitute for official reporting channels
